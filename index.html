About Me

I am a Senior Research Scientist in the Large Behavior Models division at Toyota Research Institute. My research explores how tactile and haptic sensing can enhance foundation models for contact-rich manipulation in unstructured environments.

Over the past decade, I have worked across whole-body tactile sensing, soft robotics, and Large Behavior Models (LBMs) for robot manipulation, previously serving as Robot Data Lead and Tactile Perception & Control Lead at TRI. My current research focuses on haptic-informed foundation models and multimodal LBMs for humanoid manipulation.

My key contributions include the Soft-bubble (Punyo) gripper and PolyTouch sensor systems. My work spans 50+ publications (900+ citations), multiple patents, and awards including Best Paper at ICRA 2025, IEEE RA-L 2020, and Best Student Paper Finalist at RSS 2017.

I hold a PhD from the University of Zurich, MS from KAIST, and BE from Anna University. I am driven by building robotic systems that enhance human capability and independence.
